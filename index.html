STARTUP SCRAMBLE

TUESDAY, OCT 28, 2014

An inspirational evening of art, music, talks, and socializing with the hopes of generating more citizen entrepreneurs while highlighting the accomplishments of our existing community members.

  • Learn from experienced entrepreneurs. Attending will provide you tools to help turn your idea into a business.

  • Experience the art, music, and food, that make Buffalo famous. We're taking over Allen St. from Symphony Circle to Elmwood Ave.

  • The best part of startup culture is the people. Introduce yourself to the movers and shakers - you might meet your co-founder.

Event Schedule

Starting at 5:30 pm, Startup Grind will be hosting Rohini Srihari at Karpeles Manuscript Library to kickoff the evening. Bringing dozens of community leaders, organizers, and entrepreneurs to the area.

From 5pm - 10pm venues along Allen St. will host different community groups and companies. These participating organizations will provide entertaining and educational programming to attact people to the venues. Artists and musicians will create a festival atmosphere throughout Allentown to compliment the educational components.

Check back for an interactive schedule on the evening of the event.

43North Week

Startup Scramble is part of 43North Week, a six-day celebration of innovation and entrepreneurship in Buffalo, N.Y. from Oct. 24 to Oct. 30, 2014 that builds up to the intense finals of the world's largest business idea competition. Learn more at 43north.org/week.

Keynote Speaker

Rohini Srihari

Rohini Srihari is an entrepreneur, scientist and educator. She has been involved with language technology companies for over a decade. She has worked extensively with the US Government, specifically the Department of Defense in developing innovative multilingual text mining solutions. Most recently, she founded Content Savvy, a company offering "big data" content enrichment and analytics solutions for various markets, including healthcare and customer service and support. Content Savvy was recently acquired by SmartFocus. Dr. Srihari is on the faculty of the Dept. of Computer Science and Engineering at the University at Buffalo.
